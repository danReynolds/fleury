Same data, four ways — all live in your browser. Compare the line weights and
markers to decide the flagship look.

## Braille line — 1px hairline

The original thin stroke. Highest resolution, but reads as a dotted trail in a
sparse mono font.

<FleuryExample id="linechart.lab.braille1" cols={58} rows={15} />

## Braille line — 2px band

The same braille, drawn one pixel thicker so the dots fill into a continuous
band. Portable default; heavier in a dense font.

<FleuryExample id="linechart.lab.braille2" cols={58} rows={15} />

## Octant line — solid

Unicode 16 octant glyphs: a solid, gap-free line at braille resolution. Needs a
Unicode-16 font (this site ships JuliaMono) or a kitty/Ghostty terminal.

<FleuryExample id="linechart.lab.octant" cols={58} rows={15} />

## Gradient area — a separate `AreaChart`

The filled look is different enough that it's its own widget, `AreaChart`
(sharing `LineChart`'s engine under the hood). A solid, gradient-shaded fill
of block columns — the most striking, and uniquely universal: block glyphs
render solid in any font.
